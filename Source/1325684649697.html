Non-Standard 4D Dosimetry

Via the list box in this pane, you determine whether additional settings or calculations are necessary for calculation of an OCTAVIUS 4D dose volume:

  • -None
    No additional adjustments or calculations are necessary.
    Default setting
  • -Non coplanar 4D dosimetry
    Calculation of a dose volume for non-coplanar irradiation
  • -Off-axis 4D dosimetry
    Calculation of a dose volume for irradiation of a rotation unit not positioned at the isocenter
  • -4D dosimetry for multiple energies
    Calculation of a dose volume when measurements performed at different energies are added

For further information, refer to section Further Calculations for 4D Dosimetry.

OCTAVIUS 4D Phantom

To reconstruct the dose in a phantom, the relative electron density Electron density relative to water of the phantom material must be known. For the OCTAVIUS 4D phantom material, this density is given as 1.016.

Some treatment planning systems (TPS) use a different value for the relative electron density. If this is the case, the depth dose curves of open fields at a fixed gantry position that are calculated with the TPS will slightly deviate from the curves that are reconstructed with the OCTAVIUS 4D system. This effect will be corrected if you adjust the value of the relative electron density in the TPS or in VeriSoft. For the reconstruction of IMRT plans, this is of less importance but it is helpful when introducing your OCTAVIUS 4D system.
For further information on determining the relative electron density of your TPS, please refer to the Technical Note D913.200.11 published by PTW.

Generate PDD File

In the Generate PDD File pane, you can create the PDD files required for 4D dosimetry. Here, the individual depth dose curves will be merged into one PDD file.
