Measurement Settings

Measurement tab of the Measurement Options dialog

On the Measurement tab, you will find the following options depending on the selected detector array:

Zeroing Time

Duration of the zeroing procedure (in seconds)
Default:
40 s
Hint

In contrast to VeriSoft < 5.0, you now have to set the total duration of the zeroing procedure.

Zeroing Reminder

Validity period (in hours) that determines the zeroing intervals
Default:
8 h

Dose Integration Time

Measuring time for integration measurements. The dose will be integrated over this period of time.

For measurements with the rotation unit or inclinometer, the maximum adjustable measuring time is limited on the basis of the expected file size. Additionally, the maximum adjustable measuring time depends on the set interval time (see table below).

Interval time

(0 … 600) s
200 ms

To ensure the maximum accuracy of the calculated dose in the volume for IMAT or VMAT dose measurements, PTW recommends a measuring time of ≤ 600 s and the shortest interval time. If other settings are selected, you will be alerted by a message. By clearing the corresponding check box, you can deactivate the message until the software is restarted.

Interval Time

After each measurement interval a new set of measurement values (one for each channel) is established.
Default:
400 ms
Valid values:
100 ms, 200 ms, 400 ms, and 800 ms

The maximum adjustable measuring time depends on the set interval time (also refer to Dose Integration Time).

Hint

Depending on the selected detector array and settings for data transfer, some of the interval times may not be available.

Hint

At low dose rates, longer interval times may result in a better signal-to-noise ratio. On the other hand, longer interval times reduce the maximum dose rate allowed.

Trigger Measurement

(not for 2D-ARRAYseven29 and 2D-ARRAY XDR)

If this option is selected, the measurement will be controlled by an external trigger signal.

A trigger measurement only makes sense in case of very low pulse repetition frequencies (PRF).
A typical use of the trigger measurement is an online profile representation at 6 Hz or 12 Hz.

Corrections

(not for OCTAVIUS Detector 1000 SRS)

Reference temperature for air-density correction

Scanned Beam

(only for XDR devices)

Select this option when measuring scanned proton and heavy ion beams.

Drift Suppression

(only for XDR devices)

  • Activate or deactivate the drift suppression by means of the Drift Suppression check box.
  • When the drift suppression is selected, enter a threshold for each measuring range, Low and High.
  • Default:
Low:
30.0E-06
High:
300.0E-06
  • With the Default button, you can restore the default threshold settings.

For more information on drift suppression and determination of the thresholds, please refer to section Drift Suppression.
