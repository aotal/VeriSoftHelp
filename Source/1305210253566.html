Changing the Grayscale Display

When moving the mouse cursor over the Contrast tab, a pop-up window with a slider will be displayed on the left side of the matrix. This slider can be used to change the grayscale display.

With the default setting, i.e., full length of the slider, the grayscale values are evenly distributed over the entire measuring range: the minimum value is represented by white, and the maximum value is represented by black.

Adjust the slider length, i.e., the bandwidth of the measuring range, by moving the arrows beside the slider ends, while holding the left mouse button down. The covered range is defined by the position of the slider. Values above or below the set range will be black or white.

If the cursor is hovered over one of the arrows beside the slider ends, the corresponding minimum or maximum measured value of the matrix will be displayed.

Hint

The selected grayscale range will not be saved with the matrix.

The selected grayscale distribution does not affect the generated diagrams.

When you open another matrix, the setting of the slider is retained. To restore the default grayscale display, reset the upper and lower arrows.

Example:
The image contains a very high level of gray but the lower values are to be reviewed in detail (e.g., bright X-ray film with black needle pricks). You block out the dark areas by moving the upper arrow downward, which results in a better resolution of the brighter areas.
