Composing and Loading OCTAVIUS 4D Measurements

Caution

Incorrect interpretations when composing and loading measurements with the OCTAVIUS Detector 1000 SRS.

Incorrect Operation!

When composing and loading measurements with the OCTAVIUS Detector 1000 SRS, the planned and measured doses at the isocenter or the overall dose distributions may differ even though a cross-calibration of the detector array was performed, a phenomenon that is due to the detector design. Therefore, use additional means and measures for the metrological verification of treatment plans to prevent incorrect interpretations.

Notice

To prevent equipment damage, measure matrices on the gun side after rotating the detector array by 180° for large fields. This ensures that the electronic components are not exposed to radiation.

The Compose function takes this rotation into account.

Hint

Only OCTAVIUS 4D measurements can be composed. If measurements with inclinometer but without rotation unit are selected, an appropriate error message will be displayed.

Only OCTAVIUS 4D measurements performed with the same measuring device can be composed and loaded.

Two OCTAVIUS 4D measurements can be composed only if the detector array was shifted in the direction of the rotation axis.

Composing and loading of OCTAVIUS 4D measurements is not possible in the following cases:

  • -non-coplanar irradiation
  • -irradiation of a rotation unit not positioned at the isocenter
  • -irradiation with different energies

When loading OCTAVIUS 4D measurements (xcc files), the Compose function is used to calculate an OCTAVIUS 4D dose volume for larger field sizes in the Target-Gun direction. The resolution of the composed measurement remains the same.

With this function, two overlapping OCTAVIUS 4D measurements are composed, and the overlap is symmetrical with respect to the isocenter. Shift the rotation unit by means of the treatment table for this purpose.

The shift of the detector array must ensure that the detectors are superimposed in the overlapping area and that the following conditions are met:
  • -maximum shift:
  • OCTAVIUS Detector 729 / 1500
110 mm
  • OCTAVIUS Detector 1000 SRS
45 mm
  • -The shift must be a multiple of the following values:
  • OCTAVIUS Detector 729
10 mm
  • OCTAVIUS Detector 1500
5 mm
  • OCTAVIUS Detector 1000 SRS
2.5 mm

The two OCTAVIUS 4D measurements are composed when loaded. The OCTAVIUS 4D dose volume calculated from the composed measurements can be saved in VeriSoft.

  • The OCTAVIUS 4D measurements are designated as follows:
  • -Face Out measurement:
    normal orientation of the rotation unit (normal rotation direction, gearbox facing gun)
    rotation unit shifted in Target direction
  • -Face In measurement:
    rotation unit rotated by 180° (reversed rotation direction, gearbox facing target)
    rotation unit shifted in Gun direction

  • Create a treatment plan for a long phantom (e. g., 50 cm).
  • In the measuring window, perform the following two OCTAVIUS 4D measurements. Be careful not to close the measuring window between measurements.
    • Align the rotation unit with the isocenter first.
    • Using the treatment table, place the rotation unit in a position where it is shifted in the Target direction by the desired shift value. With this arrangement, the gearbox of the rotation unit will be irradiated but not the electronic components of the detector array.
    • The measuring window will indicate the Face Out orientation, which is the normal orientation of the rotation unit (gearbox faces gun).
    • Start a measurement and deliver the plan.
    • Save the measurement in the measuring window in the xcc format.
    • Hint

      PTW recommends adding the shift direction and the shift value to the file name.

    • Set the gantry at 0°.
    • Rotate the rotation unit by 180°.
    • Align the rotation unit with the isocenter first.
    • Using the treatment table, now place the rotation unit in a position where it is shifted in the Gun direction by the desired shift value. With this arrangement too, the gearbox of the rotation unit will be irradiated but not the electronic components of the detector array.
    • In the measuring window, select the Face In orientation for the rotation unit rotated by 180° (gearbox facing target).
    • Start another measurement and deliver the plan.
    • Save the measurement in the measuring window in the xcc format.
    • Hint

      PTW recommends adding the shift direction and the shift value to the file name.

    • Select Cancel to exit the measuring window.
  • To compose and load an OCTAVIUS 4D measurement A or B, select menu File → Data Set A/B → Compose.
  • The Open Data to Compose dialog (refer to Fig. Open Data to Compose dialog) will be displayed.
  • Open the Face Out measurement by clicking the Open #1 button.
  • This will deactivate the Open #3 and Open #4 buttons.
  • Open the Face In measurement by clicking the Open #2 button.
  • In the TG Offset input box, enter the shift value unsigned in [mm].
  • For OCTAVIUS 4D measurements, a preview will not be shown but the loaded data will be identified with the Face Out and Face In icons:
Open Data to Compose dialog with two loaded OCTAVIUS 4D measurements
  • Confirm the selected measurements with the OK button.
  • The Select an angle range dialog will be displayed. It shows the values of the time and angle ranges of the Face Out measurement.
  • Perform the required settings as described in section Loading OCTAVIUS 4D Measurements and Calculating the OCTAVIUS 4D Dose Volume.
  • The OCTAVIUS 4D measurements will be composed and the OCTAVIUS 4D dose volume will be calculated.

For further information on composing and loading matrices, refer to section Composing and Loading Matrices.
